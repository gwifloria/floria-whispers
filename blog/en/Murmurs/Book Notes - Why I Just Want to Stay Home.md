### Natural Light and Happiness

> Compared to artificial light sources, most people prefer natural light. Who doesn't like having a big window next to their workstation? Trees are more pleasant than bricks. These preferences aren't just determined by aesthetics‚Äîthey're directly related to psychological well-being.

> A large study by the World Health Organization on European housing and health conditions showed that insufficient daylight or poor window views increased residents' likelihood of depression by 60% and 40% respectively.

### Human Perception and Design

> Why aren't architects interested in people anymore? Why don't architecture schools teach anything about people? Why don't I and my colleagues consider how the buildings we design will affect how people feel?
>
> Cities are designed from a bird's-eye view from above. Urban design should focus on how the city is perceived by the human body, not how it looks from above.

#### üí° Consumption and Happiness

- The sweetness of cheap things you'll quickly forget, but the bitterness of poor quality will linger in your heart
- We usually buy new things because of fantasies associated with them
- Getting others' recognition is because of our lives, not our possessions
  - Having stories to tell is better than just displaying cold objects
- If you don't buy things, you can invest the saved money; with more money, you can work less and have more time to do happier things
- Home space is for living, not for hoarding desires
- The biggest threat to capitalism is: everyone lives happily ever after with no desire to go out and buy new things.
  - In other words, from capitalism's perspective, staying home comfortably and happily is a form of rebellion.
